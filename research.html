Federated Learning

Federated learning is a framework for training machine learning models collaboratively across multiple devices or organizations while keeping data decentralized and private. Our goal is to develop communication-efficient and privacy-preserving algorithms that enable large-scale collaboration without compromising performance. We leverage advanced techniques, including adaptive first-order and zeroth-order methods, to train models effectively even under limited bandwidth, partial client participation, and heterogeneous data distributions. By bridging theoretical insights with practical algorithms, we strive to make federated learning more scalable, robust, and applicable to real-world systems.

    5. Multi-Objective Learning

    Multi-objective learning focuses on training models to optimize multiple, often competing goals simultaneously. Our research emphasizes designing algorithms that balance trade-offs, adapt to conflicting objectives, and deliver robust, practical solutions. By combining theoretical insights with applied techniques, these approaches enable models to perform effectively across diverse criteria and applications, including healthcare, autonomous systems, recommendation engines, and resource-constrained environments.
